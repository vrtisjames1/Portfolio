Get to know me!

James L. Vrtis is a Developmental Engineer for the Air Force Life Cycle Management Center. His primary responsibilities include project maangment of a maintenance scheduling software used by 60k Air & Space Force users. James L. Vrtis enlisted in the United States Air Force in December 2011. He graduated from AF Basic Military Training, Lackland Air Force Base, Texas in February 2012. He subsequently completed Airfield Systems Technical Training in November 2012. His first assignment was as an Airfield Systems Technician at the 99th Communications Squadron, Nellis AFB, Nevada. He was then later assigned to the 57th Operations Support Squadron, Nellis Air Force Base, Nevada as an Airfield Systems Journeyman. In July 2014 he volunteered for a deployment working as Force Protection to the 386th Civil Engineering Squadron, Ali Al Salem, Kuwait. He later returned to the 57th Operations Support Squadron in January 2015. He applied for a position with the Air Force Flight Standards Agency (AFFSA) and was assigned to the Pacific Regional Maintenance Center (PRMC) at Yokota Air Base, Japan in July 2016. He was the first Staff Sergeant to be assigned to the PMRC. He qualified for a Team Chief position within 12 months of his arrival, and was responsible for maintaining $12 million of equipment across the Pacific Air Force. He separated from Air Force Active Duty and transitioned to the Minnesota Air National Guard in July 2018. His current assignment with the LCMC Digital Domain.
