My Biography

James L. Vrtis is a Developmental Engineer for the Air Force Life Cycle Management Center, Digital Domain, Hanscom Air Force Base (AFB), Massachusetts. His primary responsibilities include project and team management of a maintenance scheduling software used by over 60 thousand Air & Space Force users. James L. Vrtis enlisted in the United States Air Force in December 2011. He graduated from Basic Military Training, Lackland AFB, Texas in February 2012. He subsequently completed Airfield Systems Technical Training in November 2012. His first assignment was as an Airfield Systems Technician at the 99th Communications Squadron, Nellis AFB, Nevada. He was then later assigned to the 57th Operations Support Squadron, Nellis AFB, Nevada as an Airfield Systems Journeyman. In July 2014 he volunteered for a deployment working as Force Protection to the 386th Civil Engineering Squadron, Ali Al Salem Air Base (AB), Kuwait. Upon his return, he applied for a position with the Air Force Flight Standards Agency and was assigned to the Pacific Regional Maintenance Center (PRMC) at Yokota AB, Japan in July 2016. He was the first Staff Sergeant to be assigned to the PMRC. He qualified for a Team Chief position within 12 months of his arrival, and was responsible for maintaining $12 million of equipment across the Pacific Air Force. He separated from regular active duty Air Force and transitioned to the Minnesota Air National Guard in July 2018, while obtaining a degree in Petroleum Engineering and participating in the Det 610 Air Force Reserve Officer Training Corps (AFROTC), commissioning program. Upon completion of the AFROTC program in May 2022, he commissioned abck into the regular Air Force as a Second Lieutenant.
